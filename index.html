I'm Alex Kern, a software developer and computer science student at UC Berkeley. For over 10 years, I've been programming robots and crafting web applications. I focus on building awesome software in Clojure, Ruby, and JavaScript.

I currently work as an associate at Alsop-Louie Partners, where I connect Berkeley students to the greater entrepreneurial ecosystem. This summer I worked on fraud-detecting machine learning algorithms at Apple. Previously, I interned for NASA's Jet Propulsion Laboratory, working on the Mars Science Laboratory and developing novel data storage techniques. Before attending Berkeley, I was the lead developer at Artcentric Networks, a Los Angeles web startup. In my spare time, I hack on open source projects, all of which can be found on my GitHub.
